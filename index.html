Ballas Gang

The Ballas Gang is an organized street gang that originated in the United States of America, specifically in South Los Santos, during the late 1990s. The gang was formed in response to social neglect, street violence, and the lack of protection in underprivileged neighborhoods.

The founding members were young men raised in the same area, shaped by the same struggles and losses. United by loyalty and survival, they created a structured organization with one clear objective: to protect their territory and establish dominance on the streets of Los Santos.

Their primary activities include:

  • Drug trafficking
  • Illegal weapons distribution
  • Territorial control and street enforcement

A long-standing rivalry with the Grove Street Families has defined much of the Ballas‚Äô history. This conflict led to repeated street wars over territory and influence, resulting in losses on both sides and reinforcing a culture of retaliation and discipline.

The internal structure of the Ballas Gang is strictly hierarchical:

  • OG (Original Gangster): The supreme leader and final decision-maker
  • CO-OG: Second-in-command, responsible for operations
  • Members: Enforcers and protectors of the territory

The rules of the Ballas Gang are absolute:

  • Loyalty above all
  • Respect for the chain of command
  • Betrayal is met with permanent consequences

In the streets of Los Santos, the color purple needs no explanation. Its presence sends a clear message: The Ballas are active, and their territory is not to be crossed.
